About

< full stack family man >

I am a full-stack family-man who recently took a giant leap of faith when I packed my family up and relocated with my wife and our two sons from San Diego to Austin. We moved to Austin to pursue my dreams of studying web development at MakerSquare.

The three-month journey through the MakerSquare Immersive Program was undeniably beneficial, and although it proved to be very challenging at times, I loved every minute of it. I am most proud of learning new languages, frameworks, and technologies at a highly-accelerated pace in addition to pairing with some of the most intellectual and dedicated people I have ever met in my life. More importantly, during my time at MKS, I was able to sample what it would be like to work in a problem-solving, collaborative, and tech-driven environment.

When I am not immersed in code, I love spending time with my wife and sons. We go on "family dates" and explore the city of Austin. My interests consist of writing, minimilastic art, hip-hop music, design, film-editing, and boxing (watching, not doing).

Recently, I was blessed with the privilege and honor of joining the Interactive Services department as an Associate Web Developer for an amazing company, Charity Dynamics. Here, we are given a wide range of national non-profit client work and our goal involves allowing these organizations to leverage web technologies to fulfill their missions.

So far, working with brilliant professionals and doing work that is truly meaningful, has been an awesome experience. I wake up, every day, thankful for the journey and risks we took to get here, and I look forward to seeing how my career unfolds as a Web Developer.
