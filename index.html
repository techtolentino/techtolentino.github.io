About

You can't ignore what your heart pumps for

Hello World! (cliche, I know)

My name is Randy... and I code.

I am a full-stack family-man who recently took a giant leap of faith when I packed my family up and relocated with my wife and our two sons from San Diego to Austin. We moved to Austin to pursue my dreams of studying web-development at MakerSquare. Ultimately, my career goal involves working alongside a creative team of developers and designers where I can contribute my skills and enthusiasm as a UI developer!

My previous professional experiences involved over 15-years of commitment to working with the youth in a variety of educational settings. In my last position as a Site Supervisor for a literacy program, I made it my personal mission to promote ed-tech integration throughout our school district's classrooms. Within that movement, my students and I discovered how fun web-development was; after working through a couple of lessons, I simply fell in love with the art of coding! Subsequently, with the support of my family, I decided to apply for MakerSquare and move to Austin.

The three-month journey through the MakerSquare Immersive Program has been an indescribably amazing experience, and although it was very challenging at times, I loved every minute of it. I am most proud of learning new languages and technologies at a highly-accelerated pace in addition to collaborating with some of the most intellectual and dedicated people I have ever met in my life. More importantly, during my time at MKS, I was able to sample what it would be like to work in a problem-solving, collaborative, and tech-driven environment.

When I am not immersed in code, I love spending time with my wife and sons. We go on "family dates" and explore the city of Austin. My interests consist of a well-balanced mixture of writing, minimilastic art, hip-hop music, design, film-editing, and boxing (watching, not doing).

In regards to UI development, what truly gets the blood pumpin' is the opportunity to intersect my interests with coding, all into one field! My ongoing learning trajectory includes mostly front-end work: increasing knowledge of various CSS frameworks, researching/implementing UI best practices, continuing to build side-projects, and ultimately, the conquering of JavaScript.

It's such an exciting time in my life! I look forward to sharing this momentum and positive energy with anyone who is willing to collaborate with an up-and-coming UI developer!
