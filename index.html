About

< full stack family man >

In December of 2013, I remember interviewing for an Educational-Technology position for a school district in Turner, Oregon. I was given an interesting scenario where the interviewer told me that one of their teachers wanted to make the switch from using Dreamweaver to writing her own CSS. Then, he asked me what would be my recommendation.

"I apologize, I have no idea", I replied, realizing that I had just dropped the chances of advancing my career.

The embarrassment, the feeling of failure, and the simple fact that I had no idea what 'CSS' was, honestly tore me to pieces. Consequently, in the week that followed, I made the life-changing decision to learn to code with the hopes of one day becoming a software engineer. I still have the Wordpress blog post to prove it.

The past year and a half has truly been an amazing journey, I can't emphasize enough how blessed my family is to have committed to the decision of moving to Austin. As a family, we sacrificed the comforts of being close to our loved ones and all that was familiar, just to 'make it' here in Austin.

Having zero knowledge of code, I was able to successfully learn some core fundamentals and survive Makersquare's Immersive Software Engineering program. Shortly after graduating, I joined an awesome group of developers at Charity Dynamics, where I learned how to work closely with project managers, senior devs, designers, and UX strategists, tackling real-world problems and helping non-profit organizations fulfill their missions.

Today, I am more than grateful to announce that I am a part of IBM Design as a Software Product Design Professional. I feel blessed, I believe that my personal momentum has still yet to peak, and I can't wait to continue learning and growing alongside some of the brightest problem-solvers the industry has to offer.

I am 'living proof' that anyone with the right mindset and right amount of grit, can change the game if he wants it bad enough.

I'm here to learn,
I'm here to contribute,
and I'm thankful for being able to do so.
