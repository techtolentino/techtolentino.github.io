Failure initiates the journey

In December of 2013, I remember failing an interview for an ed-tech position in Oregon.

The interviewer told me that one of their teachers wanted to make the 'switch from using Dreamweaver to writing her own CSS'. Then, he asked for my recommendation.

"I apologize, I have no idea", I replied.

My heart sunk in that moment, realizing that I had just dropped the chances of switching my career path and improving my family's situation.

That specific failure (having no idea what CSS was), led me down an interesting path, filled with curiosity and opportunity. As a result, my wife and I packed my family up and moved from San Diego to Austin, where I would learn to code and find an entirely new career.

Since that decision, our lives have changed for the best. As a family, we sacrificed the comforts of being close to our loved ones and all that was familiar, just to 'make it' here in Austin.

Less than a year and a half after that failed interview, I am more than grateful to announce that I am a Software Developer at IBM. I believe that we are very, very fortunate. And, I have strong faith that my personal momentum has still yet to peak.

God is dope.

CSS what?!? Nov '13

Failed an interview for an Ed-Tech Specialist role because I didn't know how to respond to a question about giving a teacher a recommendation for using CSS vs Dreamweaver. In that moment, I didn't even know what that meant.

The first hours of code Dec '13

Initiated the first Hour of Code program in the Chula Vista Elementary School District with my students using LearnStreet and Codecademy.

Later that same month, my wife's friend, Peter Tubig, introduced me to the idea of a applying to a developer bootcamp. That idea alone changed our lives.

Quick, learn Ruby! Jan '14

After searching for the right developer bootcamp opportunity for my family, I decided to apply to Makersquare in Austin, TX. This particular school had me learning Ruby as my first language.

Congratulations! Feb '14

A few weeks after learning to write basic object-oriented programs in Ruby, and after a rigorous application process, I was accepted into Makersquare!

I quit my job at the South Bay Family YMCA immediately.

No turning back now Mar '14

Less than 3 months later after learning my first line of code, my family packed up our dreams and moved to Austin, Texas!

Shout out MKS Jul '14

The three months at Makersquare were incredible, full of challenge, failure, and continuous learning. By the end of the program, I had gained junior-level knowledge of HTML, CSS, and Javascript, and Ruby. In July of 2014, I graduated with Cohort 6.

Officially a developer! Aug '14

Less than five weeks after graduating from Makersquare, I accepted an offer from an amazing company - Charity Dynamics. There, I was an Associate Web Developer, working with high-profile non-profit clients. I loved every minute of working with the CD team!

IBM Design Jun '15

In June of 2015, I was blessed with an opportunity to join the IBM Design Studios and accepted the offer. I completed their Designcamp onboarding program in September, and immediately deployed to the IBM CIO Office as a Front-End Developer!

A FEW SIDE PROJECTS

So far, in my professional career, I've had the privilege of working with a variety of high profile non-profit organizations, along with some pretty cool internal apps as well *cough* NDA *cough*.

The following is a list of experimental side-projects, initiated by the simple fact that there's always something new to learn in the development world.

Rhyme by Design

What happens when Design Thinking is used to make a Hip Hop album?

Follow along as the folks from Rhyme by Design journey to discover a new approach to creating dope, independent music.

This project involved using Jekyll, but as you'll see in it's About page, unfolded to an experience of learning a bunch of new workflows and technology.

Devironment

Whether starting at a new gig, or on a new machine - one problem I kept running into was trying to remember the various resources that I needed to begin coding.

Hence, I created Devironment - a simple page built using Angular, Sass, and Gulp. This allowed me to 'take my environment' with me, and cut the set up time dramatically.

Fresh Function

One of my first development projects, ever.

The problem?, hip hop and development didn't have a visible intersection. Yes, to me that was a huge problem. So, I created this dev-swag generator which gave the user to choose from various dev-related terms, and combine them onto a more street-like look.

Built on Rails, with Foundation and jQuery

Passbook

So after deciding to be a little more pro-active with our family expenses, I was totally side-tracked and began learning Angular.

Then, I had a brilliant idea - why not make a personal bill tracker prototype? Sure, tons of them exist already!, but, what better way to learn new tech, than to combine that with filling a personal need?

Built on Angular, with Firebase and Gulp.
